The original Turtle News site treated events as part of the same sprawling universe as articles, merchandise, apps, and one-off announcements. That gave the site charm, but it also made timely listings easy to miss.

The new structure separates the work without making it heavy. Event stories can still be published as articles, but they can now be tagged clearly and surfaced through search, category pages, and future event-specific landing pages.

That matters for conservation groups, school clubs, aquarium programs, and local organizers who need a simple way to share what is happening. The submission page now includes an event listing option, which means pitches can arrive with the same basic details every time.

For launch, event coverage will live in the regular news flow. As the archive grows, Turtle News can add a calendar view without changing the publishing model underneath.
