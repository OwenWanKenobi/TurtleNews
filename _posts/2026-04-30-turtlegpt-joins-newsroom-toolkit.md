TurtleGPT began as the kind of idea that could only live comfortably on Turtle News: part assistant, part shell-side columnist, and part experiment in how a small publication can build useful tools around its own voice.

The redesigned site gives those projects a dedicated home. Instead of hiding TurtleGPT, Turtle AI, Turtle Translator, and Turtle+ in a deep navigation menu, they now sit together as an Apps and Projects section with room for demos, changelogs, and future releases.

For readers, the change should feel practical. A project can be announced as a normal article, tagged as an app update, and then linked from the project directory. For editors, the workflow stays light: update one Markdown post or one data file, publish, and move on.

The first version of the projects desk focuses on clarity. Each project gets a short summary, status label, and anchor link. Future versions could add screenshots, launch buttons, GitHub links, release notes, and subscriber-only experiments.
