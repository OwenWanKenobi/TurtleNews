The Turtle News archive has always had range. One moment it nods toward evolution or conservation. The next, it swerves into dating apps, yoga, fast food, sports, or whatever strange turtle-shaped story the internet has coughed up that week.

That range is worth keeping. A professional news site does not have to sand away personality; it just needs to organize it. The new Culture section gives lifestyle, entertainment, and internet stories a place that feels intentional instead of accidental.

The result is a cleaner reader experience. Serious science can sit next to playful culture without either one confusing the other. Readers who want research can go to Science. Readers who want the charmingly odd dispatches can go to Culture. Search can still catch everything.

This is the heart of the redesign: keep the Turtle News voice, reduce the maze, and let every story type find a sensible home.
